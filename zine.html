// About The Series

Field Notes is a zine series that takes anthropological thinking and makes it useful. Not just theoretical — actionable. Each issue tackles one specific political or community topic from the ground up, combining research, personal perspective, and practical tools.

The name comes from ethnographic field notes — the raw, honest observations made outside institutional walls. That's what this series is: notes from the field, for people living in it.

Issues are pay-what-you-want with a $3 minimum. If you can pay more, you're supporting future issues and keeping this accessible to people who can't.
