// About Who
I Am

The Academic

I'm a graduate student in Cultural and Forensic Anthropology, working at the intersection of folklore, food politics, and what I call epistemic decolonization — the project of taking seriously the knowledge systems that Western academia has spent centuries dismissing.

My current research spans punk culture as living folklore, apiculture as a resilience indicator in food-insecure communities, and the forensic and folkloric traditions we use to make meaning of the dead.

The Practitioner

I'm active duty USAF enlisted and a practicing astrologer running NAOS — a consulting practice built on the thesis that astrology is not pseudoscience but a delegitimized non-Western knowledge system. The same argument runs through everything I research.

The Publisher

Field Notes is my zine series — accessible, political, community-rooted. Issue 01 is about ICE abolition and concrete ways to build and protect community. Written for everyday people, not academic audiences.
